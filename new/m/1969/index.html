1969

TECHNICS’ FIRST
INTEGRATED AMPLIFIER

STORY

Technics’ first transistor integrated amplifier, the Technics 50A, used OCL circuitry in its power amplifier section, following its successful use in the Technics 20A power amplifier. In addition, the 50A incorporated PNP-type low-noise transistors in the preamplifier section. Featuring many innovative technologies, the 50A was showcased in the marketplace. To dispel the common notion that transistor amplifiers produced “hard” sounds, the 50A was designed to emanate “warmth” through its acrylic panel with illuminated indications.
The 50A and other Technics amplifiers continued to by highly evaluated for their unique concepts and reliable technologies.
